
 
The sign in Samsung'sbooth at CES may still boast that the company has the "world's first" 102-inch plasma TV, but rivalsMatsushita Electric Industrial (AKA Panasonic) have added a whopping inch to their biggest display, giving them (fornow at least) the title of world's largest plasma. Unveiled at CES, the prototypedisplay has a 1920x1080 resolution and a 3,000:1 contrast ratio. And now that they've been beaten, we hear that Samsungis going to leave that 102-incher out with the trash when they vacate CES this weekend.
